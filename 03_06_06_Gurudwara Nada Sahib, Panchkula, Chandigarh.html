Nada Sahib Gurudwara Ji, Panchkula, Chandigarh

Gurudwara Nada Sahib marks the spot where Guru Gobind Singh Ji halted after the Battle of Bhangani in 1688, en route from Paonta Sahib to Anandpur Sahib. A devoted Sikh of the Labana community named Nadu Shah served the Guru and his entourage with food and milk. In gratitude, Guru Gobind Singh blessed that the place would be named after him and that it would become a place of pilgrimage

The site remained relatively unknown until Bhai Motha Singh of a nearby village discovered it and erected a platform (Manji Sahib) to commemorate the Guru’s stay. After 1948, control transitioned from local boards to the Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (SGPC) in 1956

Located about 15 km from Chandigarh city, along the Ghaggar River in Panchkula.

105-foot holy flag (Nishan Sahib) stands near the shrine, symbolizing Sikh sovereignty

The complex also includes a museum showcasing Sikh history, scriptures, relics, and artifacts tied to Guru Gobind Singh Ji
